1. Describe a time you needed to collaborate and develop trust from a stakeholder
================================================================================

Situation
The Chat Analysis Tool (CAT) is used by business stakeholders, including the
Digital Assistant team, to analyse customer interaction trends and support
evidence-based improvements to chatbot responses. At the time, stakeholder
confidence in the tool had reduced.

The application was unstable, required frequent manual restarts, and did not
fully meet business expectations around user access and reliability. Users could
not consistently rely on the tool when they needed it, which had created
tangible frustration and eroded trust in the service.

Task
My task was to support improvements to CAT while helping rebuild trust with
dissatisfied stakeholders. This required more than technical fixes. I needed to
understand what users actually needed, gather clear requirements, manage
expectations through the process, and ensure the final outcome addressed both
the reliability issues and the confidence gap that had developed.

Action
I began by focusing on the stakeholder relationship rather than moving
immediately to solutions. I listened to the concerns raised by business users
and clarified what success would look like from their perspective: stable
access, appropriate user permissions, fewer disruptions, and confidence that
issues raised would be followed through.

Working collaboratively with stakeholders, I gathered and refined requirements
for authentication and role-based access, ensuring that different users could
access the functions relevant to their work while maintaining appropriate
controls. During testing, I actively sought feedback, responded to issues
promptly, and supported hotfixes where needed to ensure the solution met user
expectations rather than simply satisfying the technical specification.

To address the reliability concerns, I investigated the recurring instability
and worked with the Data Lake team to identify the underlying
environment-related causes. I supported improvements to monitoring and
automated restarts to reduce the need for manual intervention and improve
day-to-day availability.

Throughout the work, I communicated progress clearly, was transparent about
risks and timeframes, and kept stakeholders involved in shaping the solution
rather than presenting them with a finished outcome. This consistency of
follow-through was as important to rebuilding trust as the technical
improvements themselves.

Result
Stakeholder sentiment shifted from dissatisfaction to confidence in the
application. CAT became more stable, access controls better reflected business
requirements, and users experienced fewer disruptions to their work. The
collaborative approach to testing and requirements ensured the changes genuinely
addressed user needs, and the visible follow-through on commitments helped
restore the working relationship on a stronger footing than before.

2. Describe a time you helped someone understand the strategic direction of your
   area or the business context of a decision
================================================================================

Situation
The Data Engineering Operations team supports data processes that need to be
reliable, traceable, and maintainable for ongoing business use. The team was
working toward more consistent delivery practices, including clearer
documentation, shared development processes, and better use of version control.

During this period, a senior technical team member left the agency, creating a
continuity risk. The challenge was not only to retain technical knowledge, but
also to help the team understand why these practices mattered to operational
reliability and sustainable delivery.

Task
I took responsibility for supporting continuity within the team and helping
colleagues understand the business context behind the technical practices being
adopted. My focus was to make the work easier to maintain, reduce reliance on
individual knowledge, and support a more consistent way of working.

Action
I built my understanding of the projects by reviewing available materials,
attending handover discussions, and asking targeted questions. I then supported
colleagues with practical guidance on development environments, repositories,
and Git-based workflows.

Rather than only explaining the steps, I connected the practices to business
outcomes. I explained that version control improves traceability, documentation
supports continuity, and consistent ways of working reduce avoidable errors and
operational risk. This helped colleagues understand how their day-to-day
technical work contributed to reliable data operations.

I also recorded walkthroughs, documented key processes, and encouraged
questions to facilitate broader knowledge sharing across the team. My approach
was to lead with clarity and practical support, ensuring people understood both
what needed to be done and why it mattered.

Result
The team maintained continuity after the staff transition and became more
confident working with the projects independently. The team retained key
knowledge, reduced reliance on any single person, and developed a clearer
understanding of how structured delivery practices supported operational
reliability.

3. Describe a time you led by demonstrating continuous growth, emotional
   intelligence or respect
================================================================================

Situation
While working with the Data Engineering Operations team, I became involved in
DevOps-related work including GitLab workflows, merge requests, pipeline
behaviour, and release processes. These practices were important to support
safer, more consistent, and more repeatable delivery. However, parts of the
process were new to me and to some colleagues, and issues arising from gaps in
understanding could create confusion or delays.

Task
I needed to develop my own capability quickly while supporting colleagues
respectfully through the same learning curve. My aim was to help resolve issues
as they arose, reduce confusion, and contribute to a more confident and
constructive working environment.

Action
I built my understanding systematically — reviewing existing repositories,
testing workflow behaviour, reading configuration files carefully, and asking
targeted questions when I needed clarification. When issues occurred, I focused
on understanding the root cause rather than making assumptions.

I supported colleagues by explaining processes clearly, sharing what I had
learned, and helping troubleshoot issues in a calm and practical way. I was
deliberate about treating problems as shared learning opportunities rather than
individual mistakes, which helped maintain trust and made it easier for others
to raise questions without hesitation.

Where I identified gaps, I suggested practical improvements such as clearer
documentation, more structured workflows, and better visibility of common
issues — with the aim of making the process easier to follow in future, not
just resolving the immediate problem.

Result
My own capability in DevOps and CI/CD strengthened meaningfully over this
period, and colleagues became more confident working within the processes
independently. The team benefited from clearer guidance, improved shared
understanding, and a more collaborative approach to resolving technical issues.
By focusing on both the technical and interpersonal dimensions of the work, the
team was better placed to handle similar challenges without escalation or
disruption.

4. Describe a time you found a solution to a technical, ethical or complex
   problem
================================================================================

Situation
I worked on the Australian Government Disaster Recovery Payment project, which
used a machine learning model to support and enhance the existing fraud
detection strategy. A key issue was that model outputs needed to be retained
reliably so they could be reviewed and traced during operational use. If outputs
were not captured consistently, there was a risk that relevant information could
be missed, reducing confidence in the model's contribution to fraud detection
activities.

The complexity was that the solution needed to work in a production environment,
meet security expectations, and preserve the model's existing behaviour. It was
not enough to make outputs visible during testing; they needed to be captured
consistently, stored appropriately, and remain reliable after deployment.

Task
I was responsible for updating the application so that model outputs could be
logged in a structured way and stored reliably in the production environment.
I also needed to test the deployment in OpenShift and review package
vulnerabilities to support cyber security requirements. My focus was to improve
traceability and reliability without introducing unnecessary change or release
risk.

Action
The first step was to clarify the operational problem: the priority was not
simply producing model results, but ensuring those results were retained in a
consistent and traceable way. A review of the application flow established where
outputs could be captured and how they needed to be stored for later use.

I updated the application so that outputs were written in a structured format
and connected to the appropriate storage configuration. I then tested the
deployment to confirm the logs were created, stored, and available as expected
in the production-like environment. This was important because changes that work
locally may not behave consistently once deployed.

Package vulnerabilities were also reviewed to support the cyber security
process, with checks confirming that any required updates did not affect the
application's expected behaviour. Throughout, changes were kept focused and
practical, risks were raised early, and modifications to the model logic were
avoided where possible.

Result
The updated implementation improved the reliability and traceability of model
outputs, reducing the risk that relevant fraud detection information would be
missed during operational use. The solution also met cyber security expectations
and provided a more reliable basis for handover to the Applied Intelligence team
for release preparation.

Ongoing engagement after handover has helped ensure the solution remains
maintainable and continues to meet operational needs as further requirements
emerge.
